BIS IS 2296 : 1992 — Class B · Modified

Recreation & Tourism
Water Quality Index

A modified Recreation and Tourism WQI built from the BIS IS 2296 : 1992 Class B standard (Outdoor Bathing — Organised). BOD was not measured at either site and is therefore excluded. The four available parameters — total coliform, pH, dissolved oxygen, and turbidity — are used in its place, providing a practical, measurement-grounded score for recreational suitability.

01 — Reference Standard
BIS Class B — Outdoor Bathing (Organised)
Class B covers water bodies intended for organised outdoor bathing, leisure fishing, boating, and contact recreation. Our WQI uses three of the four Class B parameters (coliform, pH, DO) plus turbidity as a practical visual-quality substitute for the missing BOD measurement.
B
Outdoor Bathing (Organised)
BIS IS 2296 : 1992
Total Coliform ≤ 500 MPN / 100 mL
pH 6.5 – 8.5
Dissolved Oxygen ≥ 5 mg/L
BOD (5-day, 20 °C) ≤ 3 mg/L Not Measured
Turbidity (added) ≤ 5 NTU ideal
Why turbidity? Turbidity reflects water clarity and visual quality — directly relevant to recreational perception. It replaces BOD as a practical field-measurable recreation parameter. Class B does not specify a turbidity limit, so we define a practical scoring range based on contact-recreation standards.

Reference: BIS IS 2296 : 1992 — Designated Best Use Class B (Outdoor Bathing — Organised). Source: Central Pollution Control Board. Parameters used: Total Coliform (weight 40%), pH (20%), Dissolved Oxygen (20%), Turbidity (20%). Coliform carries the highest weight because microbial contamination is the principal health risk in contact recreation. BOD (Class B limit: ≤ 3 mg/L, 5-day at 20 °C) was not measured at either site and is excluded from this index. Turbidity was added as a practical proxy for visual water quality in the absence of BOD data.
