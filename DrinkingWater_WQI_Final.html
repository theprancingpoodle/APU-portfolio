Water Quality Assessment · Bengaluru, Karnataka

Potential Drinking-Water
Source WQI

Both Varthur Lake and Hesaraghatta Reservoir are currently unsuitable for direct human consumption. This index does not ask whether the water is drinkable today — it asks a more urgent question: which lake would be easier to treat into safe drinking water if Bengaluru ever needs to rely on it? Higher scores mean less treatment effort required.

Reference Standard
BIS IS 10500 : 2012
India's national specification for drinking water. Limits used here: pH 6.5–8.5, Turbidity ≤ 1 NTU (permissible 5), TDS ≤ 500 mg/L, Hardness ≤ 200 mg/L, Nitrate ≤ 45 mg/L, Ammonia ≤ 0.5 mg/L, and zero detectable coliform in any 100 mL sample.
Why This Matters for Bengaluru
A City Under Water Stress
Bengaluru is among India's most water-stressed cities. Rapid construction is draining and encroaching on lakes that once recharged groundwater. The lakes that remain may need to serve as backup drinking-water sources in the future — making it essential to understand how much treatment each would realistically require.
01 — Final Verdicts
Treatment Burden Assessment

A score of 100 means minimal treatment effort — the water is close to drinking standards. A score approaching 0 means an enormous treatment burden — multiple complex processes would be needed before the water is safe to drink. The two lakes tell very different stories.

02 — Sub-Index Calculations
Seven Parameters, Scored Out of 100

Each parameter is converted into a score from 0 to 100, where 100 = closest to the BIS drinking-water standard and 0 = farthest from it. Parameters are weighted differently because they don't all pose the same challenge to a water treatment plant. Coliform and turbidity carry the highest weights because they represent the biggest physical burdens in raw surface water treatment.
