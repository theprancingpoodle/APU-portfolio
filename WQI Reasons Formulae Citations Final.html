Water Quality Index

Purpose-specific methodology & formulas

Reasons to develop purpose-specific WQIs
1

Different parameters have different real-world implications and dangers — some are more crucial than others, hence weighting is important

2

Varying use-cases need specific sets of parameters to be measured and compared to varying standards

3

Minimizes errors which simple averaging is prone to

4

Makes comparison among water bodies easier and enables decision-making

The WQIs and sub-indices have been designed to range from 0 to 100, with a higher score indicating better water suitability for a designated purpose.

Formulas adopted for our calculations
Final WQI
\[ WQI = \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n}(w_i \times SI_i)}{\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i} \]
Sub-index — Higher is worse
\[ SI_i = \left(1 - \frac{X_i - X_{ideal}}{X_{max} - X_{ideal}}\right) \times 100 \]
Sub-index — Higher is better
\[ SI_i = \frac{X_i - X_{min}}{X_{ideal} - X_{min}} \times 100 \]
Sub-index — Range based

\(SI_i = 100\) within acceptable range; decreases linearly outside
