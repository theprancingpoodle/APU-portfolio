Fisheries & Biodiversity
Water Quality Index

The WQI here is benchmarked against Class D of the BIS IS 2296:1992 surface water standards — the class designated for the propagation of wildlife and fisheries. Class D specifies four parameters: pH, Dissolved Oxygen (DO), Free Ammonia (as N), and BOD. Since BOD was not measured at either site, it has been omitted from the calculation. The WQI is therefore derived from the three remaining Class D parameters — pH, DO, and Free Ammonia — each of which was directly observed and maps cleanly onto the Class D criteria.

03 — Sub-Index Calculations
How Each Parameter Was Scored (0 – 100)
Each raw measurement is converted into a score between 0 and 100 — the sub-index. The direction of scoring differs by parameter type: pH must stay within a range, DO rewards higher values, and ammonia penalises higher values. All three are then combined using weights proportional to their ecological importance in Class D.
