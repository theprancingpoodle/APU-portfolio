⚡ Key Analytical Insight
Even though Varthur has higher COD and nutrient levels, it still performs better in all WQIs because the type of pollution matters — not just the amount.
Hesaraghatta has severe microbial contamination — directly eliminates it for recreation and drinking use.
Hesaraghatta's very high pH (9.34) stresses aquatic organisms and complicates treatment chemistry.
Hesaraghatta's borderline dissolved oxygen limits biodiversity and fish survival.
Different pollutants dominate different use cases — this is the core analytical takeaway of the entire study.

Conclusion
"Across all three use-specific WQIs, Varthur consistently performs better than Hesaraghatta. However, both lakes require treatment and management interventions. The results highlight that water quality is not a single concept — suitability depends strongly on intended use, and different parameters dominate different use cases."
